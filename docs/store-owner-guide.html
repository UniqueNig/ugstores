Everything you need to run your shop yourself: add products, handle orders, and more.

1 · Logging In

  1. Open your store’s admin page: yourstore.com/admin/login (I’ll give you the exact link).
  2. Enter the email and password I set up for you.
  3. You’re now in your control panel (the “admin”).
Tip: Bookmark the admin link on your phone and computer, and keep your password private. Don’t share your login — instead add staff as their own admin (Section 11).

2 · The Dashboard

The first screen shows a quick overview (sales, orders, etc.). On the left is the menu with everything you can manage: Products, Categories, Orders, Coupons, Shipping, Settings, and more.

The bell 🔔 at the top shows new orders and alerts. A number on it means something needs your attention.

3 · Categories — Do This First

Every product belongs to a category (a group, like “Dresses” or “Bags”). Create your categories before adding products.

  1. Go to Categories in the menu.
  2. Click Add, type a name (e.g. “Shoes”), and save.
  3. Repeat for each group of products you sell.

4 · Adding a Product

This is the main thing you’ll do. Go to Products → Add New Product and fill in:

  1. Name — the product’s name.
  2. Description — what it is, materials, why it’s great.
  3. Price — the selling price.
  4. Category — pick the group it belongs to.
  5. URL slug — fills in automatically from the name. Leave it as is.
  6. Images — upload photos. The first photo is the main one (shown on the shop grid); the rest appear in the product gallery.
  7. Sizes & Stock — if the product has sizes, add a row per size with how many you have (e.g. S = 3, M = 5, L = 4; or shoe sizes 40, 41, 42). If it has no sizes, just use the single Stock Quantity field instead.
  8. Colours (optional) — add a colour name, pick the colour, and upload photos for that colour. Customers can then choose a colour and the photos switch to match.
  9. Size Guide — choose Clothing, Footwear, or None (controls the size chart customers see).
  10. Extra details (optional) — Materials, Sizing & Fit, Care Instructions.
  11. Mark as New — turn on to show a “New” badge.
  12. Click Save. It’s instantly live in your shop. ✅
Photo tip: Use tall (portrait) photos, about 1000 × 1500 pixels, and shoot all of a product’s photos at the same size so the gallery looks neat. Good lighting and a clean background sell more.

5 · Editing or Removing a Product

  • Edit: Go to Products, click the product, change anything, then Save.
  • Hide instead of delete: set its stock to 0 — the shop shows “Sold Out” but keeps the page (good for items returning later).
  • Delete: removes it permanently. Use only when you’ll never sell it again.

6 · Stock & “Sold Out”

  • When an item (or a size) reaches 0, the shop automatically shows “Sold Out” and stops customers buying it.
  • Customers can ask to be emailed when it’s back. The moment you raise the stock above 0 and save, those waiting customers are emailed automatically — bringing them back to buy.
  • The Inventory page gives you a quick view to check and adjust stock levels.

7 · Orders — Your Daily Task

Go to Orders to see every order: the customer, what they bought (including size/colour), and the total.

  1. When a customer pays, the order appears and its status becomes Processing. You also get a “New order” email.
  2. Prepare/package the order.
  3. Update the status as it moves: Processing → Shipped → Delivered.
  4. The customer is kept informed; you keep a clear record of what’s done.
Tip: Stock is reduced automatically when an order is paid — you don’t adjust it manually for sales.

8 · Discount Codes (Coupons)

  1. Go to Coupons → Add.
  2. Set a code (e.g. SAVE10), the discount amount, and any minimum spend.
  3. Customers type the code at checkout to get the discount.

9 · Delivery Options (Shipping)

Go to Shipping to set how you deliver and what it costs. Add options like:

  • Standard delivery — e.g. ₦3,000
  • Express delivery — e.g. ₦7,000
  • Store pickup — ₦0

These appear for customers to choose at checkout.

10 · Store Settings

Go to Settings to manage:

  • Store name and currency
  • Contact email — where customer messages and order alerts go
  • WhatsApp number — powers the “Order on WhatsApp” button on products
  • Notifications — turn order/email alerts on or off

11 · Other Things You Can Manage

  • Reviews — see and manage customer reviews of your products.
  • Testimonials & Team — the quotes and people shown on your homepage / About page.
  • Subscribers — people who joined your newsletter.
  • Customers — your registered customers.
  • Admins — add a staff member with their own login (only give access to people you trust).
  • Profile — change your own name and password.

12 · What Your Customers Experience

So you understand the full flow:

  1. They browse your Shop and open a product.
  2. They pick a size/colour, add it to the cart, and go to checkout.
  3. They pay securely by card, transfer, or USSD (via Paystack) and get a confirmation email.
  4. You get a new-order alert and fulfil it (Section 7).

13 · A Simple Daily Routine

  • Check Orders — fulfil new ones and update their status.
  • Restock items running low; restock anything that sold out.
  • Reply to any contact messages (sent to your contact email).
  • Add new products as they arrive.
